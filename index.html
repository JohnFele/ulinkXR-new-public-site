FAQ

How does coding and robotics fit into the South African curriculum?

Ulink integrates Coding, Robotics, and immersive XR experiences directly into CAPS and IEB-aligned lessons. Learners can move from physical coding tools to interactive digital creation in Delightex and explore their own 3D worlds in XR, reinforcing ICT, Mathematics, Natural Sciences, etc. through hands-on discovery.

Do teachers receive training and support?

Yes. Every UlinkXR package includes teacher training, lesson planning, and technical support. Educators are guided step-by-step on how to blend robotics, coding, and VR into their teaching, with ongoing access to Ulink’s digital resource hub and live support team.

What age group is Matatalab Tale Bot Pro suitable for?

Matatalab Tale Bot Pro is designed for Early Childhood Development (ECD) and Foundation Phase learners. It’s screen-free and uses playful coding cards to teach sequencing and logic through storytelling and problem-solving.

What coding tools are available for older infrastructure?

Older learners can advance to DelightEx Edu Pro, where they code in block-based or Python environments and create interactive 3D simulations. With UlinkXR, they can step inside their creations using VR headsets, transforming abstract coding exercises into fully immersive learning experiences.

How does the VR option enhance learning?

Through UlinkXR, learners can explore science experiments, historical worlds, or their own digital projects in lifelike VR using UlinkXR and Meta Quest 3 headsets. This immersion deepens understanding, boosts engagement, and builds future-ready digital confidence beyond traditional classroom methods.

What happens if a school doesn't have strong technical infrastructure?

UlinkXR is designed to scale with your school. You can begin with robotics and browser-based coding, and when ready, expand into VR learning spaces. Ulink conducts a full “tech check” to ensure smooth implementation at every stage of your digital transformation journey.

Do learners need their own devices?

No. UlinkXR experiences are designed for shared learning. Coding can run on standard browsers, tablets, and Chromebooks, while VR sessions can rotate between learners using DelightEx and Meta Quest 3 headsets, ensuring inclusive, collaborative participation without needing one device per child.

What are the costs involved?

Ulink offers flexible rental and purchase options, allowing schools to start their digital journey affordably. Packages can include robotics kits, DelightEx coding licences, UlinkXR headsets, and full teacher training, with rentals starting from as little as R699 per month.

How can coding and robotics benefit learners beyond academics?

Together, Ulink’s coding, robotics, and UlinkXR environments build essential 21st-century skills such as creativity, collaboration, problem-solving, and digital fluency. Learners do not just learn about technology; they learn through it, preparing for future careers and real-world challenges.
